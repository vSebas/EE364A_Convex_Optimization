\section*{A17.18 Option price bounds}

We use arbitrage-free pricing to find bounds on the price of a collar option.

\textbf{Setup:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Risk-free asset with payoff $r = 1.05$ in every scenario, price $= 1$
    \item Underlying stock with current price $S_0 = 1$
    \item $m = 200$ scenarios with $S^{(i)}$ uniformly spaced from $0.5$ to $2$
    \item Call options: $K = 1.1$ (price $0.06$), $K = 1.2$ (price $0.03$)
    \item Put options: $K = 0.8$ (price $0.02$), $K = 0.7$ (price $0.01$)
    \item Collar with floor $F = 0.9$ and cap $C = 1.15$ (price unknown)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Arbitrage-free pricing framework:}

A state-price vector $\pi \in \mathbf{R}^m$ satisfies $\pi \succeq 0$ and gives the ``price'' of each scenario. For a portfolio to be arbitrage-free, there must exist such a $\pi$ where the price of each asset equals $\pi^T v$, where $v$ is the vector of payoffs.

Let $V \in \mathbf{R}^{m \times n}$ be the payoff matrix where $V_{ij}$ is the payoff of asset $j$ in scenario $i$. The arbitrage-free condition requires:
\[
V^T \pi = p
\]
where $p$ is the vector of known asset prices.

\textbf{Assets and payoffs:}

For scenario $i$ with stock price $S^{(i)}$:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Risk-free: payoff $= 1.05$
    \item Stock: payoff $= S^{(i)}$
    \item Call $K=1.1$: payoff $= \max(0, S^{(i)} - 1.1)$
    \item Call $K=1.2$: payoff $= \max(0, S^{(i)} - 1.2)$
    \item Put $K=0.8$: payoff $= \max(0, 0.8 - S^{(i)})$
    \item Put $K=0.7$: payoff $= \max(0, 0.7 - S^{(i)})$
    \item Collar $F=0.9$, $C=1.15$: payoff $= \min(C, \max(F, S^{(i)}))$
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Finding price bounds:}

To find the range of arbitrage-free prices for the collar, we solve two LPs:

\textbf{Lower bound:}
\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{minimize} \quad & v_{\text{collar}}^T \pi \\
\text{subject to} \quad & V_{\text{known}}^T \pi = p_{\text{known}} \\
& \pi \succeq 0
\end{aligned}
\]

\textbf{Upper bound:}
\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{maximize} \quad & v_{\text{collar}}^T \pi \\
\text{subject to} \quad & V_{\text{known}}^T \pi = p_{\text{known}} \\
& \pi \succeq 0
\end{aligned}
\]

\textbf{Results:}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{l|c}
Quantity & Value \\
\hline
Lower bound on collar price & $0.9850$ \\
Upper bound on collar price & $1.0173$ \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The arbitrage-free price range for the collar with $F = 0.9$ and $C = 1.15$ is:
\[
\boxed{0.9850 \leq p_{\text{collar}} \leq 1.0173}
\]

\textbf{Interpretation:}
The collar acts like a ``capped and floored'' version of the stock. It provides downside protection (floor at $0.9$) while giving up upside potential (cap at $1.15$). The price range is fairly tight, reflecting that the traded options provide good information about the state-price distribution.
